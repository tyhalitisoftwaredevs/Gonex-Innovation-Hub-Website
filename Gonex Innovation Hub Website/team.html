Meet the team

The people driving Gonex forward.

Young, committed, and from this community. Every one of us has a stake in making this work.

Our founders

Built by the community

Gonex was founded by people from Sterkspruit who decided to stop waiting for opportunity and start building it.

Yongama Goso
CEO & Founder

Yongama founded Gonex with a vision to close the opportunity gap in the Joe Gqabi District. He leads strategy, partnerships, and program development, with a background in information systems and community development.

Mzoli Somi
Co-Founder

Mzoli brings operational discipline and entrepreneurial experience to Gonex. He oversees day-to-day hub operations and leads the entrepreneurship development program, helping members from idea to launch.

Yamkela Kulu
Co-Founder

Yamkela leads digital skills training and community outreach at Gonex. His background in education and technology enables him to design programs that are practical, accessible, and genuinely transformative for members.

Asenath Oziel Tyhaliti
Co-Founder

Asenath handles communications, marketing, and stakeholder engagement for Gonex. He is the voice of the hub online and offline, and ensures the community always knows what's happening and how to get involved.

Our team

The people on the ground

Beyond the founding team, Gonex is growing with people who bring specific skills, community roots, and earned trust to the work.

Phumelelo Fobozi
Youth & Community Coordinator

Phumelelo leads youth recruitment, school partnerships, and community outreach with a kind of credibility that cannot be hired — he is a published author, spoken word poet, and qualified language practitioner who has spent years building genuine trust with Sterkspruit's schools, families, and young people through his own work. His national recognition for his contribution to Pan-African literature and his expertise in translation and interpreting mean Gonex can reach and communicate with communities in the language, register, and cadence that actually resonates. He does not bring the hub to the community — he is already of it.
